Content Description

The collection features books, zines, videos, and recorded interviews covering the graffiti and street art movement, including nearly 600 zine issues across 345 zine titles, 156 books, 41 early graffiti VHS/DVD "magazines" (6 DVD and 35 VHS tapes) from the early 90s, and approximately 52 audio tapes preserving interviews with ca 40 writers collected between 1991 and 1996 (44 cassette tapes and 8 micro cassettes). Writers interviewed include Futura, Iz the Wiz, Phase 2, Vulcan, SEEN, and Zephyr. Also interviewed are those knowledgeable about or involved in aspects of graffiti activity in NYC, including Pat DiLillo, founder of the Phun Factory and the 5Pointz project in Queens, author and photographer Jack Stewart, journalist and documentarian Sacha Jenkins, former New York City mayor Ed Koch, and David Gunn, former president of the New York City Transit Authority (1984 to 1990).
Biographical / Historical

Joe Austin is a scholar of graffiti and street art and the author of Taking the Train: How Graffiti Art Became an Urban Crisis in New York City, 1970-1990 (Columbia, 2001). He retired in 2022 as Associate Professor, Emeritus, from the University of Wisconsin, Milwaukee's Department of History.
