ORGANIZATIONAL HISTORY

The First Baptist Church of Romulus was established in 1795 with seven members. The first church was established in 1808 and was rebuilt in 1849. The church joined the Chemung Association in 1796 but left it in 1804 to join the Cayuga Association only to leave again in 1820 when they helped establish the Seneca Association.
COLLECTION DESCRIPTION

This collection contains eight transcribed items arranged in chronological order including: two sets of church meeting minutes, 1818-1846, 1847-1873; one set of covenant meeting minutes, 1873-1898; and five membership lists, 1826-1849, 1849-1880, 1861-1898, 1874-1888, and 1895. (This collection is a microfilm copy of typed transcripts located in the Seneca County Courthouse filed under "miscellaneous historical record."

"First Baptist Church Romulus. A continuation of our church Records M 2d 1818." Church meeting minutes, 1818-1846 . 57 pp.

Scope and Contents
Minutes contain: names of persons excluded from communion; names of persons admitted to the church and form of admission, i.e. letter, certificate, examination; names of persons who received letters of dismissal; minutes of a special Baptist council held in Romulus; names of individuals appointed trustees; confession of faith, pp. 19-21; and a discussion of a protracted meeting held between March and April, 1843, p. 48.
Item #2
"First Baptist Church Romulus, N.Y. Book I." Membership list, 1826-1849 . 10 pp.
 1826-1849
Scope and Contents
Membership list regularly includes: members' names, the manner and dates of their admissions, manner and dates of dismissals, and indications of baptisms and deaths.
