Biographical / Historical

The Screamers were an American punk rock group formed in 1975 that participated in the first wave Los Angeles, California punk scene. Often categorized as electropunk, their sound included a synthesizer (ARP Odyssey) and electric piano (Fender Rhodes). Other punk musicians and bands have cited the Screamers as a key influence. The group never released an official recording of any kind, although several bootleg recordings of rehearsals or live performances have since appeared; they have been called one of the great unrecorded groups in rock history. Artist Gary Panter's logo for the band, a stylized cartoon of a screaming head with spiked hair, became one of the most recognizable images to emerge from punk rock.
COLLECTION DESCRIPTION

Collection includes original art, photographs and fliers, a 1977 fan club application, press kits, press releases, files of handwritten lyrics, letters, press clippings and magazines, including TOLO, the Franklin High School (Seattle, WA) Newsletter with articles authored by Tommy Tibbits (Gear), a notebook that belonged to Tomata du Plenty (1978), touring, publicity and business plans, guest lists, fan club mailing lists, plans to release a debut album in video form, and other business records and correspondence
