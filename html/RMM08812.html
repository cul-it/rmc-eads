Content Description

Magazines, flyers, correspondence, and ephemera from the career of A.J. Weberman, including his first edition bootlegs of "Tarantula" and the first and only vinyl press of the "Weberman Tapes" (vinyl record titled "Bob Dylan vs. A.J. Weberman" containing a phone interview between the two).
Biographical / Historical

A.J. Weberman is an American writer and political activist and self described "underground cat and garbologist." Weberman became obssesed with Bob Dylan in the late 1960s, when he attempted to decode the Nobel Prize winning musician's lyrics with a crude "hemeneutical" approach he termed "Dylanology." He printed and distributed the first bootleg of Bob Dylan's experimental novel "Tarantula". When Bob Dylan stoped writing political music, Weberman formed the Dylan Liberation Front (DLF) in order to free Bob Dylan from himself. Weberman appeard in the national spotlight when he started going through Bob Dylan's trash in order to find clues to his lyrics.
