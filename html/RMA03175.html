INFORMATION FOR USERS
Cite As:

Robert J. Smith Papers, #14-25-3175. Division of Rare and Manuscript Collections, Cornell University Library.
Scope and content

Correspondence, research, articles, course material and lectures pertaining to Dr. Smith's study of Japan. Including articles and information about Japanese emperors, ancestor worship, the Japanese in Brazil, historical demography and material concerning the student protests at Cornell in 1969, correspondence with David Nathan Meyerson, and scrapbooks with newspaper clippings from World War I and World War II, material concerning Smith's participation in the Army Specialized Training Reserve Program and programs from various plays from the 1940s.
Scope and content

Includes correspondence with Ella L. Wiswell concerning the study of the Japanese village of Suye-mura.

Series XVI. Newspapers
Box 33
Copies of Mainichi and the Nippon Times concerning post-World War II
 1940s-1950s
Processing Information
Includes a condensed, representative selection of newspapers previously located in boxes 33-35 with headlines of historical importance related to the United States occupation of Japan and the Cold War, including the Tokyo War Crimes Tribunal, the creation of Japan's constitution, Supreme Commander for the Allied Powers (SCAP) policies, Japanese politics, and the Korean War. Also includes some supplements with visual materials capturing the spirit of the time. Boxes 34 and 35 have been removed from the collection.
Series XVII. Army Specialized Training Reserve Program (ASTRP)
Scope and Contents
These materials cover the years from Robert Smith's enlistment in the US Army Reserve in July 1944 to his service in the occupation of Japan in 1946. In the Army Specialized Training Reserve Program (ASTRP) and Army Specialized Training Program (ASTP) at Carnegie Tech, University of Minnesota and Yale University.

The following folders contain Ella L. Wiswell's correspondence with a variety of individuals and institutions. Most of the correspondence concerns the 1935-1936 study of the Japanese village of Suye Mura conducted with her first husband John Fee Embree and her subsequent visits there in 1951 (following his death in 1950) and 1968 and 1985 with her second husband Frederic Wiswell.
