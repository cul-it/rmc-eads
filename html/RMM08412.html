COLLECTION DESCRIPTION

Posters, broadsides, memorabilia, etc., pertaining to Lincoln, particularly to his death and funeral. Contents include a Lincoln campaign song, 1864; a Thanksgiving proclamation, State of Wisconsin, April 12, 1865; an item listing the order of the funeral procession at obsequies of President Lincoln, 1865; an item concerning another funeral procession, held at Danville, April 19, 1865; an item on a public meeting in Boone County, Missouri, April 22, 1865; an item concerning a lecture by J.H. Surratt, Dec. 30, 1870, on a plot to kidnap Lincoln; and a poem by Edwin Markham, 1929, titled Lincoln, the man of the people.
"Lincoln and Liberty!!, Tract No. 14, New York, Sept. 18, 1860" political pamphlet issued by the Young Men's Republican Union of the City of New York for Lincoln and Hamlin.
Broadside: "The Irrepressible Conflict or The Republican Barge in Danger," published by Currier & Ives, 1860.
Also, early assassination broadside: "Crutch Extra!!! Important from Washington. Death of President Lincoln. The Assassin Escapes." printed for sick and wounded Union troops at Anapolis and one of the earliest printed descriptions of the assassination. Emancipation Proclamation, General Orders No. 139. Issued from the War Department, Washington, D.C. Signed in print by Abraham Lincoln, President; William H. Seward, Secretary of State; and Lorenzo Thomas, Adjutant General (3pp.). "Golden Sayings of Abraham Lincoln," broadside with 18 quotes from Lincoln. "Little 'Mac'" Copperhead ticket attacking George McClellan's credentials during the 1864 campaign. Harper's Weekly on Lincoln's assassination, May 20, 1865.
Two Abraham Lincoln Centennial souvenir postcards, 1909.
Emancipation Proclamation stamp and first day cover, 2013.
