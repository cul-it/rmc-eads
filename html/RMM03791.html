ORGANIZATIONAL HISTORY

The Union for Radical Political Economics (URPE) was founded in 1968 by graduate students and faculty from the University of Michigan, Harvard University, and Radcliffe College. The organization promotes an interdisciplinary approach to political economics that considers political science, sociology, and social psychology. URPE's annual activities include a winter meeting as part of the Allied Social Science Association (ASSA) and a summer conference. URPE publishes a quarterly journal, "Review of Radical Political Economics" (RRPE), and a bi-monthly newsletter.
COLLECTION DESCRIPTION

Includes correspondence, memoranda, editorial board minutes, and other records of the Union for Radical Political Economics (URPE) and of its publication, the "Review of Radical Political Economics" (RRPE). Other acronyms commonly used in the collection include American Economic Association (AEA), Allied Social Sciences Assocations (ASSA), Steering Committee Monthly Reports (BAG).
