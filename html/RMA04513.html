Content Description

Collections contains papers documenting the research and teaching of Gesa Valk. Includes material on German Expressionism and Georg Kaiser, sculptur and printmaker Ernst Barlach, and author Ludwig Harig. Also some items relating to Wolfgang Paulsen.
INFORMATION FOR USERS
Preferred Citation

Gesa Valk papers, #14-18-4513. Division of Rare and Manuscript Collections, Cornell University Library.
Biographical / Historical

In 1972 Gesa Valk earned a PhD in German Studies and Comparative Literature from Cornell, and was a senior lecturer there until 2001. For 30 years she pursued her research interest, which was the life of Georg Kaiser, a German Expressionist playwright. Gesa is the author of numerous publications and was known as the foremost authority on Georg Kaiser.
Collection is closed until processed.

 1978
Collection of German theater programs
 Circa 1970-1990
Scope and Contents
Playbills and theater programs from German productions, mostly in West and East Berlin. Covers a range of productions of German and international theater (in German), primarily 20th-century work (with Brecht and Heiner Müller heavily represented), also plays of German Classicism and German-language productions of ancient drama, etc. The programs illustrate the evolution of important theaters of East Berlin (e.g., Berliner Ensemble, Deutsches Theater, Volksbühne) from the last decade of the German Democratic Republic through the early years of German unification. The wide range graphical styles, production values, and paper quality indicate the diversity of the theater scene of the period as well as Cold War disparities.
