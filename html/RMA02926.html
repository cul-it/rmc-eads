ORGANIZATIONAL HISTORY

The Division of Campus Life and the Division of Academic Programs merged in late 1990 to become the Division of Academic Programs and Campus Life and was headed by Larry Palmer whose title went from Vice President of Academic Programs to Vice President of Academic Programs and Campus Affairs. It was not a complete merger because some departments of Academic Programs were assigned to the colleges and some departments under the Division of Campus Life (such as Police, Health Services and Athletics and Physical Education) were not incorporated in the merger, but rather were assigned to the then Senior Vice President. In 1994, Susan Murphy became Vice President of Student and Academic Services, replacing the Vice President of Academic Programs and Campus Affairs. Some departments within the Division, such as the Museum, Cornell Press, Cornell in Washington,and ROTC, were reassigned to the then Vice President, John Weisenfeld. In 1995, Susan Murphy took responsibility for Athletics and University Health Services, but the Division did not change names.
