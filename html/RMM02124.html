DESCRIPTIVE SUMMARY

Title:
Hervey DeWitt Griswold papers, 1897-1959.
Repository:
Division of Rare and Manuscript Collections
Collection Number:
2124
Abstract:
Dr. Griswold's "Autobiographical Notes, prepared for his descendants" (247 pp., 1938-40); two printed copies of Griswold's studies of Hinduism; five typed or printed items pertaining to his career as a missionary at Forman Christian College, Lahore, India (now Pakistan); pamphlet "Sketches of My Indian Childhood" by Frances Griswold Wooddell (116 pp., n.d.); genealogical pamphlet on the Griswold family of Dryden, New York; and typescript poems by Hervey De Witt Griswold (28 pp.).
Creator:
Griswold, H. D. (Hervey De Witt), 1860-1945.
Wooddell, Frances Griswold, 1900-
Quanitities:
.2 cubic feet.
Language:
Collection material in English
BIOGRAPHICAL NOTE

Missionary, professor of philosophy, Presbyterian minister.
Hervey DeWitt Griswold received his Ph.D. from Cornell in 1900.
COLLECTION DESCRIPTION

Dr. Griswold's "Autobiographical Notes, prepared for his descendants" (247 pp., 1938-40); two printed copies of Griswold's studies of Hinduism; five typed or printed items pertaining to his career as a missionary at Forman Christian College, Lahore, India (now Pakistan); pamphlet "Sketches of My Indian Childhood" by Frances Griswold Wooddell (116 pp., n.d.); genealogical pamphlet on the Griswold family of Dryden, New York; and typescript poems by Hervey De Witt Griswold (28 pp.).

Box 1
Manuscript: "Autobiographical Notes, prepared for his descendants," written by Dr. Griswold between Aug. 1938 and Apr. 1940 [see pp. 1, 246]. 247 pp., written on lined loose-leaf notebook paper. Chapter contents as follows:
Scope and Contents
  1. I. "Early Days and First School," pp. 1-7.
  2. II. "The Dryden School and Youthful Poetry," pp. 7-36.
  3. III. "Miscellaneous Experiences at High School," pp. 36-48.
  4. IV. "Union College 1881-1883," pp. 49-59.
  5. V. "First Term, Junior Year," pp. 55-99.
  6. VI. "Second Term, Junior Year," pp. 99-129.
  7. VII. "Third Term, Junior Year," pp. 129-154.
  8. VIII. "Fourth Year, Union College," pp. 154-163.
  9. IX. "Union Theological Seminary," 1885-1888, pp. 163-169.
  10. X. "Oxford and Berlin," [1888-1889, 1889-1890], pp. 169-179.
  11. XI. "In India, 1891," pp. 179-247. [Actually includes entire career in India; work for Ph.D. at Cornell, 1899-1900; family; travels in Europe and Near East; people met, etc.]
