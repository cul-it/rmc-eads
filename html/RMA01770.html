BIOGRAPHICAL NOTE

Electrical engineer. Lucien L. Nunn founded the Telluride Power Company in Telluride, Colorado, and the Telluride Association, a student-managed organization which provides housing and other assistance for intellectually gifted high school and college students at Cornell University and at Deep Springs, California.
COLLECTION DESCRIPTION

Correspondence; photographs and negatives; maps, blueprints, and specifications; clippings; addresses and lectures; printed material; reports; constitution, by-laws, and amendments; financial records; applications; minutes; and other papers documenting the career of Lucien L. Nunn, his founding and direction of the Telluride Association, and the development of the electrical power industry in the United States, including information concerning the Telluride Motor Company, Telluride Power Company, and Ontario Power Company. Also, manuscript history of Telluride by Orville Sweeting. Black and white photographs of Telluride Association Conventions, 1911-1960 and CD with pdf copies of photographs. DVD titled "Hydro Power" from the Rocky Mountain PBS series Colorado Experience, season 2 (2014).
Letters from L.L. Nunn to Wallace Cook.
Family letters, primarily to L. L. Nunn,1863-1938, 1881-1882 (bulk) include original letters, many with typescript transcripts, as well as photocopies. Correspondents include his mother, Miriam Kendall Nunn, his brothers Josiah J. Nunn and Frederick Thomas Nunn, and especially his sister Nellie. 1846 Bible given to L. L. Nunn by his grandmother, Mary Towler Kendall and an 1867 Concordance owned by Nunn. Also, Nunn family genealogy sheets, copies of "snippets" from the diaries of Alice Bird (L. L. Nunn's niece), and three photographs.
Group photographs of members of the Cornell Branch of the Telluride Association, 1910-1960.
RELATED MATERIALS

Related collections: Deep Springs College Records, #6428; Oral History Interviews for Deep Springs College and Telluride Association, #37-4-4042; and John Burchard papers, #6793, http://resolver.library.cornell.edu/cgi-bin/EADresolver?id=RMM06793. See also the book The Electric Edge of Academe.
