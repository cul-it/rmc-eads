DESCRIPTIVE SUMMARY

Title:
Anna Elsbree papers, 1788-1953.
Repository:
Division of Rare and Manuscript Collections
Collection Number:
1600
Abstract:
Includes genealogical correspondence, 1922-53; mimeographed Elsbree family letters and other papers; letters and other papers of Jabez and Lewis Beers relating to the New Jerusalem Church of Danby, 1815-74, and to the Ithaca and Owego Turnpike Company, 1810-20; attendance records and other papers of the Tornado Hook and Ladder Company, No. 3, Ithaca, 1843-1928; notes and clippings on history of Ithaca and Tompkins County; commencement ball in New Haven; invitations; cemetery records from Tompkins County; autograph album, account and recipe book, and photograph album of Llewellyn Elsbree; many family and other photographs; broadsides and pamphlets; and much miscellaneous material on local history and genealogy.
Creator:
Elsbree, Anna
Quanitities:
4.5 cubic feet.
Language:
Collection material in English
COLLECTION DESCRIPTION

Includes genealogical correspondence, 1922-53; mimeographed Elsbree family letters and other papers; letters and other papers of Jabez and Lewis Beers relating to the New Jerusalem Church of Danby, 1815-74, and to the Ithaca and Owego Turnpike Company, 1810-20; attendance records and other papers of the Tornado Hook and Ladder Company, No. 3, Ithaca, 1843-1928; notes and clippings on history of Ithaca and Tompkins County; commencement ball in New Haven; invitations; cemetery records from Tompkins County; autograph album, account and recipe book, and photograph album of Llewellyn Elsbree; many family and other photographs; broadsides and pamphlets; and much miscellaneous material on local history and genealogy.

Scope and Contents
1824, Dec. 20, New Jerusalem Society - announcement of annual meeting, signed by Elbert Curtis, Sec'y; 1822, April 21, Danby - copy of letter "Dear Sister" on religion and work of the society, unsigned (Jabez Beers?); 1822, May 21, Danby, copy of letter sent to Annual Convention, Philadelphia regarding "present unhappy division of sentiments which prevail respecting the pastoral relation of the Minister to his Society;" 1824, Jan. 10, record of resolution to build church and to start a subscription; 1825, Jan. 19, list of subscribers to building fund; 1831, July 25, copy of agreement signed by members to subscribe specified sums of money for erecting a public library "to consist of book in which the Doctrines of the New Jerusalem Church are contained and taught, and also books in which those doctrines are questioned or disputed;" 1843, Oct. 10, deed, Lewis Beers to the First New Jerusalem Church of the County of Tioga (now Tompkins) land on which church was erected; undated, list of subscribers to purchase organ
