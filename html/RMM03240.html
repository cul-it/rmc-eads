ORGANIZATIONAL HISTORY

The Central New York Regional Medical Program was a project funded by the U.S. Department of Health, Education and Welfare. Its goals were to increase quality and access to health care, while moderating cost. Under the guidance of a Regional Advisory Group, it worked to foster cooperative efforts to improve health care and provide grants for innovative projects designed to meet its goals. The program was terminated in 1976.
COLLECTION DESCRIPTION

Includes document files, financial materials, evaluation materials, committee minutes, proposals for funding from health programs and projects, project files, librarian files, historical files and grant applications of the Central New York Regional Medical Program.
