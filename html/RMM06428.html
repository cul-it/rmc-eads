ORGANIZATIONAL HISTORY

Deep Springs College, founded in 1917 by power and mining magnate L. L. Nunn, is located thirty miles from the nearest town, in a high desert valley of eastern California. Mr. Nunn wished his students to learn the value of service and practical knowledge as well as be able to work uninterrupted by the distractions of modern society and learn that freedom requires living in accordance with the ideals and duties of one's community. Each of the 26 male students experiences a broad seminar-style education based on the "three pillars of Deep Springs": academic pursuits, the labor program, and self-governance. Following their time at Deep Springs most students transfer to other institutions of higher learning to earn college degrees.
COLLECTION DESCRIPTION

Trustees records include minutes, 1923-1996; reports, 1960-1975; and correspondence, 1913-1984. Deep Springs student body records contain minutes, 1917-1999, co-education studies reports, 1979-1994. Also student, community, committee and class projects, 1933-1990's; Telluride Association newsletters, 1915-1983; Telluride Association convention minutes, 1911-1999; and Deep Springs Alumni newsletters, 1966-1999. These materials include minutes, reports, correspondence, projects, photograph albums, and newsletters. Includes a manuscript copy of "A History of Deep Springs" with chapters by various authors.
Also, works compiled by Denis E. Clark, 2001, including "Telluride Power: A Brief History of the Early Days," "Nunn Archive Image Index," and three CD-Rs: DEC Nunn Archive Images, vol. 1 and vol. 2 (images of the early days of the Telluride Power Company obtained from various sources), and Deep Springs Archive Photos (images scanned by students at Deep Springs College, mostly Telluride Power Company, but some early Deep Springs)
Custodial History

In 2011, the Deep Springs Board of Trustees voted to admit female students but a legal challenge was brought by two dissenting trustees, Kinch Hoekstra and Edward Keonjian. After more than five years of litigation, the California Supreme Court resolved the issue in 2017, ruling in favor to permit the admission of women as students

Legal counsel records
 1917-2017
This series contains Deep Springs College's legal files, the bulk of which were kept to attorney Christopher L. Campbell, Esq. of Baker, Manock, and Jensen in Fresno, California. While many files relate to general legal matters, the majority pertain to Deep Springs College's decision to go co-educational after almost a century of being a men-only institution.
Processing Information
Arranged by client matter number.
Custodial History

In 2011, the Deep Springs Board of Trustees voted to admit female students but a legal challenge was brought by two dissenting trustees, Kinch Hoekstra and Edward Keonjian. After more than five years of litigation, the California Supreme Court resolved the issue in 2017, ruling in favor to permit the admission of women as students
