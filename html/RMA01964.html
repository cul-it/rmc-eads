The Cornell University Ceramics, Glassware, and Metalware collection consists of items commissioned by the University, fraternities and societies, and departments. ORGANIZATIONAL HISTORY

The original set of Cornell plates was created in 1932, based on a design created by E. Stewart Williams (Class of 1933) for the Baird Prize "Place Plate" artwork competition for Design 112, February 1932. The winning design was used by the Wedgwood Company for a set of twelve plates depicting Cornell scenes. Originally created in mulberry, copper green, and Staffordshire blue, they were advertised in the Cornell Alumni News and sold through the Cornell Alumni Corporation. They continued to be advertised and sold through at least 1943. In 1950, they were re-issued.
COLLECTION DESCRIPTION

The Cornell University Ceramics, Glassware, and Metalware collection consists of items commissioned by the University, fraternities and societies, and departments. Materials include correspondence concerning Cornell Wedgwood china; full sets of Cornell Plates, made by Wedgwood, in Mulberry and blue, with twelve different designs, depicting Cornell scenes and buildings including the Crescent, Sage Chapel, Baker Laboratory, Myron Taylor Hall, Goldwin Smith Hall, Balch Halls, the Clock Tower, the War Memorial, McGraw Hall, Willard Straight Hall, the Ezra Cornell Statue, and Sibley Dome. Also includes a full set of Wedgwood mulberry and blue cups and saucers and an Incomplete set of green saucers and cups.
The collection also contains mugs, cups, and glasses from different class years, reunions, sororities, fraternities, and departments, as well as commemorative plates.
Also, correspondence concerning Cornell Wedgwood china.

MU 1080 MU-1080
Cornell University stein
Scope and Contents
Designed by Frederick L. Ackerman Cornell Class of 1901 and William Henry Miller. Made at Mettlach by Villeroy & Boch, Germany. Made exclusevley for Rothschild Brothers, Ithaca, New York. Has lyrics to the Alumni Song and depicts campus buildings. An owl is on the lid.
MU 4734 MU-4734
Cornell University Book Beer Stein
 1897
Scope and Contents
Manufactured in Mettlach, Germany by Villeroy & Boch. Depicts books with titles and names of Cornell professors such as "Study of Insects" for John Comstock, "Vetrinary Advisor" for James Law, other names include, Nichols, Gage, Schurman, Wilder, Caldwell, Roberts, Atkinson, Bailey, and Dennis.
