Scope and content

Pamphlets, circulars, brochures, reports, programs, and other publications on various aspects of Cornell University, including academics, buildings, events on campus, athletics, student life, alumni and alumnae. Includes reports to the New York State Assembly on Cornell University activities, mailings to alumni, syllabi for courses, publications on Cornell fraternities or sororities, information manuals for students and for faculty, brochures on local buildings offering housing or dining to students; programs for concerts, banquets, races, and other events; speeches, articles, and letters by Cornell faculty or staff; a circular letter from President Jacob Gould Schurman on the typhoid epidemic, 1903; a program for the exercises at the laying of the cornerstone of the University Library building, October 30, 1889; and other Cornell-related items. An additional volume (volume 16) includes a report on an archeological expedition to Asia Minor, an article on the retirement of Professor Simon Henry Gage, and a June 1908 of the Alumni Ooze, focusing on the reunion of the Class of 1903.
