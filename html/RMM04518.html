BIOGRAPHICAL NOTE

Richard David Cushman received an AB in 1962, MA in 1968 and Ph.D. in anthropology from Cornell University in 1970. His main work was with the Yao tribal people in northern Thailand, an ethnic group which is mainly located in south China. The Yao have a strong literary tradition, expressed in Chinese characters but in Yao language; their religious tradition is apparently something close to Taoism. One of Cushman's main preoccupations during field work in Thailand in the early 1970s was to collect Yao texts. He collected these by two main methods: 1) he hired older men to copy their religious texts into hardbacked notebooks; and 2) when he could obtain access to the original manuscripts, he photographed them. He also tape recorded Yao music and chanting on reel-to-reel audio tape.
COLLECTION DESCRIPTION

Notebooks, files relating to the Yao language, reprints in English and Chinese on the Yao Rebellion of 1832, and Cornell University anthropology course materials. Also reel-to-reel and cassette tape recordings of Yao music and chanting.
The collection also includes a set of 23 Yao/Mien paintings (ca. 1950) collected by Richard Cushman from northern Thailand, includes one Yao/Mien shaman headdress.
Alternative form available:

The audio tapes in this collection have been digitized and are available upon request with advance notice of two (2) business days.
