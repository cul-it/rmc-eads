ORGANIZATIONAL HISTORY

The Ithaca Rotary Club was founded in 1914 by Edward G. Wyckoff, a prominent Ithaca businessman inspired by the Rotary movement and its ideals of fellowship and service. The club was the first Rotary club in the country founded without sponsorship from an established club, and it did not fully join the broader Rotary International organization until 1915. Ithaca Rotary has sponsored programs such as the Student Exchange, and has supported numerous organizations and causes, both throughout the Ithaca community and internationally. Ithaca Rotary has also sponsored the formation of additional Rotary clubs, including the Ithaca Sunrise Rotary Club, the Ithaca-Cayuga Rotary Club, and clubs in Dryden and Trumansburg, NY. Women have been members of Ithaca Rotary since Rotary International first allowed women in 1987. For more information, please see Margaret Hobbie's A BRIEF HISTORY OF THE ITHACA ROTARY CLUB (1995), Box 17, folder 15.
COLLECTION DESCRIPTION

Records created or assembled by the Rotary Club of Ithaca, New York. The collection includes correspondence, reports, minutes, memoranda, bylaws, secretarial and presidential files, membership records, Board of Directors records, annual reports, financial records, photographs, audiovisual material, oral histories, and a scrapbook. Publications of the Ithaca Rotary Club include bound and unbound issues of THE ROTARY CLUB NEWS (weekly publication), and pamphlets. The collection contains additional publications such as newsletters, convention proceedings and books from Rotary International, newsletters from Rotary Districts, publications from other Rotary clubs and a songbook. Audiovisual material consists of a videotape, CDs, floppy disks, DVDs and a phonograph. Ephemera and memorabilia include plaques, certificates, news clippings, and miscellaneous artifacts.
The majority of material in this collection dates from the 1950's through 1980's. A few items date back to the founding and early years of the organization. More recent material consists largely of photographs, meeting minutes and issues of THE ROTARY CLUB NEWS.

Series IV. Secretaries
Scope and Contents
Contains the files assembled by secretaries of the Ithaca Rotary Club. These files cover a broad range of subjects, including membership and attendance, committees, club programming, material related to specific presidents and treasurers, and material pertaining to Rotary in a broader sense, including Rotary International, the Rotary Foundation, and the various Rotary districts of which Ithaca Rotary has been a member.
General Secretaries, 1946-1974
Scope and Contents
This series begins with material from Earl Atkinson (1923-1946) and Frank Philipps (1946). It also includes material from a time of secretarial transition (1968-1974). When Secretary Frank R. Bliss died suddenly in 1970, several secretaries held the post in quick succession, including Clarence W. Mitchell, William H. Allen, Jr., and Robert "Bob" Head. Unlike other secretarial files, which are arranged according to secretary, the organizer of these files sorted them by years and topics, combining material from Bliss and the secretaries who followed him within each file. Finally, the series also includes one box of files (1944-1967) organized alphabetically rather than by subject. For instance, a file labeled "T" could contain material from the club treasurer, correspondents whose name begins with T, etc. This material is largely from Ollie Jones' tenure, but also includes some material from Frank Philipps' time as secretary.
