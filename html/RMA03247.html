Biographical / Historical

On March 14, 1974 the Board of Trustees voted to abolish the Graduate School of Nutrition and the Department of Human Nutrition and Food in the College of Human Ecology and to establish the Division of Nutritional Sciences, an intercollege unit within the Colleges of Agriculture and Life Sciences and Human Ecology.

Preventing Childhood Obesity: Online Training Course
 2006-2013
Scope and Contents
This online course was designed to train Cooperative Extension and other community nutrition and youth development educators nationally in a new approach to preventing childhood obesity in their communities. The focus was on an ecological approach that involved assessing behavioral, environmental and other factors that contributed to obesity and then determining interventions to change those factors in order to support healthy food and activity behaviors in the community. By the end of the course, participants had created a four-part action plan describing a collaborative approach to preventing childhood obesity customized for their community.
Scope and Contents
The course was offered as a facilitated 6 week course and participants were encouraged to enroll as teams from the same community. In 20 sessions, over 750 people from 50 states and territories and 23 countries enrolled. Of these, 635 people completed the course.
Biographical / Historical
The course was developed and delivered in partnership with eCornell. The course development was funded by a Faculty Innovation in Teaching grant from Cornell and the delivery and evaluation was funded primarily by a grant from USDA's Agriculture and Food Research Initiative (AFRI).

Programming for Infant and Young Child Feeding: Online Training Course
 2012-2019
Scope and Contents
This online course was developed to enhance the competencies and build the capacity of UNICEF staff and their counterparts worldwide who are involved in Infant and Young Child Feeding (IYCF) programs in low-and middle-income countries. The content covered IYCF program development, implementation and evaluation. The course was developed in collaboration with UNICEF and was offered for free. More than 14,500 Cornell NutritionWorks members from 181 countries registered for the UNICEF course and more than 4,700 people from 150 countries completed the course.
Biographical / Historical
The course development, delivery, and evaluation were funded by a grant from UNICEF.
