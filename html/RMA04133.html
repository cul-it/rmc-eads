BIOGRAPHICAL NOTE

Per Pinstrup-Andersen is the H. E. Babcock Professor of Food, Nutrition and Public Policy, the J. Thomas Clark Professor of Entrepreneurship, and Professor of Applied Economics at Cornell University and a Danish economist and policy maker who won the 2001 World Food Prize for personally initiated the research effort which enabled several governments to reform their food subsidy programs and dramatically increase food availability to the severely impoverished. He has a B.S. from the Danish Agricultural University, a M.S. and Ph.D from Oklahoma State University.
