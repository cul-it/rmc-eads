Content Description

The collection contains documentation from various sources (NGOs, government agencies, project brochures, publications, and reports) covering reconciliation efforts in Cambodia in the post-Khmer Rouge era gathered by Laura McGrew during her PhD research.

Biographical / Historical

Laura McGrew is a consultant in the fields of human rights, reconciliation, and transitional justice in Cambodia (where she's worked since 1987) as well as Rwanda, Sri Lanka, Afghanistan, Nepal, and Bosnia. In addition to graduate degrees in medical science from Emory University and public policy from Johns Hopkins University's School of Advanced International Studies, McGrew received her PhD from Coventry University in 2011 upon the completion of her dissertation, "Reconciliation in Cambodia: victims and perpetrators living together, apart."
