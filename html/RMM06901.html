Scope and content

The records of the Lehman Alternative Community School in Ithaca, NY, which include administrative records, teaching materials, yearbooks, newsletters, flags, pictures, and other files. Also present are many publications and other materials on alternative education.
INFORMATION FOR USERS
Preferred Citation

David Lehman's Alternative Community School Records, #6901. Division of Rare and Manuscript Collections, Cornell University Library.
Biographical / Historical

Dave Lehman founded the Alternative Community School in 1974, and served as its principal until he retired in 2004. The combined middle school and high school is a public school, part of the Ithaca City School District, that operates based on alternatives to traditional curriculum and school governance philosophies. After his retirement, the school was renamed the Lehman Alternative Community School.

David Lehman
 1956, 1972-2006
Scope and Contents
Records stored by David Lehman, which include administrative records, publications, realia, photographs.
Lacking in original order, this sub-series has been intellectually arranged in alphabetical order.
Biographical / Historical
Lehman was the founding principal of LACS, having been installed at the newly created New Junior High Program (NJHP) in 1974. He oversaw its growth as it incorporated new grades, eventually becoming the Alternative Community School (ACS) in 1981. The school was renamed the Lehman Alternative Community School (LACS) following his and his wife's retirement in 2004 (Judy Lehman served as school secretary since the school's founding).
