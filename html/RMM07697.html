DESCRIPTIVE SUMMARY

Title:
Chai Feldblum papers, 1975-1997.
Repository:
Division of Rare and Manuscript Collections
Collection Number:
7697
Abstract:
Materials of Professor Chai Feldblum relating to the effort by the Campaign for Military Service to assist President Clinton in his stated desire to lift the ban on gay service members. These materials include all documents used to lobby Congress and raise public awareness in 1993, as well as internal documents detailing efforts to propose alternatives to the Don't Ask Don't Tell policy. Also, files regarding the Employment Non-Discrimination Act (ENDA).
Creator:
Feldblum, Chai.
Campaign for Military Service
Quanitities:
19.2 cubic feet.
Language:
Collection material in English
BIOGRAPHICAL NOTE

Professor Chai Feldblum was the Legal Director for the Campaign for Military Service (CMS).
COLLECTION DESCRIPTION

Materials of Professor Chai Feldblum relating to the effort by the Campaign for Military Service to assist President Clinton in his stated desire to lift the ban on gay service members. These materials include all documents used to lobby Congress and raise public awareness in 1993, as well as internal documents detailing efforts to propose alternatives to the Don't Ask Don't Tell policy.
Also, files regarding the Employment Non-Discrimination Act (ENDA).
Access for research purposes only.
One folder of correspondence has been RESTRICTED until 2019. This folder is temporarily stored in the Accession File.
INFORMATION FOR USERS
Cite As:

Processing Information

Where possible, original folder headings have been retained. Folders labeled "Miscellaneous" contain information papers, comments by Navy officers, retired officials and veterans, various background papers, and other ban-related miscellany.
