Content Description

Scrapbooks of John M. Ferris III, Cornell University class of 1951. Contains clippings, ephemera, correspondence, and photographs relating to his time at Cornell.
Biographical / Historical

John M. Ferris III graduated with a BS from Cornell University in 1951 and a PhD in 1956.

Description
Date
Box 1
Scrapbook
 1947-1951
The spine of this scrapbook is partially dismembered and the pages inside are no longer bound.
Scope and Contents
The box contains an Alpha Tau Omega fraternity 1951 class picture and Ivy football tickets. Included in the scrapbook are news clippings, photographs, and napkins from the wedding of Ferris to his wife, Virginia J. Ferris (née Rogers) and memos from Purdue University, where both Ferris and his wife became professors of Entomology. Ferris included many photographs and ephemera from their travels across the Midwest, New York, and Canada; maps, travel guides, dried flowers, and hotel room stationery among them.
Box 2
Scrapbook
 1947-1951
This scrapbook is warped and overstuffed. Pages contain annotations and are prone to tearing.
Scope and Contents
Scrapbook contains photographs of John M. Ferris III, Alpha Tau Omega, Spring Weekend, football, the Big Red Band, the construction of Mann Library, The Dennis Club, housing floor plans, the Golden Nemotode Lab in Hicksville, NY, and even an aerial view of campus. Ephemera from the College of Agriculture, class activities (freshman through senior year), Freshman Club, Fall 1947 rush, The Cornell Dramatic Club, Willard Straight Hall activities, Christian Science organization, Spring Weekend, Farm and Home Week, concerts, Octagon musicals, and athletics. Also included are letters from Ferris to his family and memos from his future wife, Virgina J. Ferris.
