BIOGRAPHICAL NOTE

David A. Burr was the son of David H. Burr, Surveyor General of Utah Territory.
COLLECTION DESCRIPTION

Included is a diary of journey from Washington, DC, to Utah Territory in 1855 in which Burr describes the steamboat trip, life on the trail, the appearance of the land, Indians, and horse thieves. There are also letters written from Utah (1858-1864) discussing details of the conflict between the Mormons and the U.S. government, clippings, and school essays. Includes a number of photographs (including of Brigham Young) and Burr's sketches of scenes in Utah. Also included is an autograph album containing approximately 350 signatures collected by Mrs. David H. Burr and later members of her family, beginning in 1848 and continuing through 1969. Over three quarters of the autographs were collected in Washington, DC in the late 1840s and early 1850s. Included are the signatures of ten presidents (including Lincoln and Roosevelt), and many senators and representatives of the Thirty-first Congress, as well as many other public figures.

Box 2 Folder 1
Diary of David Burr
 1855
Scope and Contents
Burr writes of his journey from Washington DC to the Utah Territory. Included are descriptions of a steamboat trip along the Mississippi, of life on the trail, of the flora, fauna, and topography of the regions passed through, and of incidents involving Indigenous people and horse thieves.
Box 1 Folder 9
Diary of David Burr
 1857-11-02-1857-11-16
Scope and Contents
In these loose pages, Burr describes joining a military party and encountering Mormons. Back at camp, delegates sent by Brigham Young arrived to negotiate the return of prisoners and allowed Mrs. Mogo (wife of Charles Mogo) to be reunited with her husband and leave the Church. Burr further describes the military tactics of Brigham Young and some of his preachings. He reports that the Mormons had killed forty apostates who were trying to flee to California, and says he will write of Charles Mogo's escape from Salt Lake City. Burr also writes of the capture of Mormon prisoners.

Box 2
Autograph album
 1848-1969
Scope and Contents
Album containing approximately 350 signatures collected by Mrs. David H. Burr and later members of her family, beginning in 1848 and continuing through 1969. Over three quarters of the autographs were collected in Washington, DC in the late 1840s and early 1850s, from the House of Representatives. Included are signatures of: Dolly Madison, Louisa Catherine Adams, Grace Greenwood, Fredrika Bremer, James Buchanan, Frederick D. Grant, William Jennings Bryan, William B. Preston, William Marcy, William R. King, A.P. Butler, Dan Sturgeon, Aylett Buckner, Charles Golden Atherton, A. Johnson, Jefferson Davis, Abraham Lincoln, Joseph Crane Hartzell, Millard Filmore, William Taft, Winston Churchill, among many others.
