Annotated student assembly minutes, resolutions, a speech, and other files relating to the struggle of gay, lesbian, and bisexual students to define a strong community for itself on the Cornell campus during the 1992-1993 academic year. BIOGRAPHICAL NOTE

Lesbian, Gay and Bisexual At Large representative on Cornell's Student Assembly and leader of the Men Supporting Men support group for gay/bi/questioning men coming out of the closet in 1992-1993. In the US Presidential election, where Democratic candidate Bill Clinton had promised to end the ban on gays in the military if elected. The specter of AIDS hung over the community as well, as many students had friends and relatives who had succumbed to the disease. Barrios remembers that students perceived that gay issues were beginning to be talked about earnestly by the public at large, but that this also caused tension among the substantial segment of the population that remained intolerant towards gays. On campus, there were 12 LGBT student groups, chief of which was the Lesbian, Gay and Bisexual Coalition run by Demetri Moshoyannis and Rebecca Rugg. The Lesbian, Gay and Bisexual At Large seat on the Student Assembly that Barrios filled had not seen much use since its creation.

Scope and content

The Joseph L. Barrios papers document major stories that occurred on campus during the early 1990's, including rally in response to anti-gay graffiti chalked on campus, LGBT program house for student living, Student Assembly resolution that married couples housing violates Local Law C and university should open housing to gay and lesbian couples, and the national story that ROTC was asking cadets to sign a statement saying that they agreed with the military's anti-gay policy (first came to light at Cornell). The material consists primarily of newspaper articles, but includes some photographs depicting Demetri Moshoyannis, Andy Garcia, Kate Brody, and Joseph Barrios. Also included is Barrios' keynote speech for Lavender Graduation, May 4, 2018 - "Time's Up. Take Action."
