DESCRIPTIVE SUMMARY

Title:
Lawrence I. Grinnell papers, 1930-1977.
Repository:
Division of Rare and Manuscript Collections
Collection Number:
2649
Abstract:
More than 70 motion picture films, photographic and written materials regarding ornithological trips taken by Lawrence Grinnell from 1938 to 1971; correspondence and a pamphlet, "The 1957-1958 New Zealand-Australia Expedition of the Laboratory of Ornithology"; materials pertaining to his dissertation A Study of the Common Redpoll, a notebook of Cornell ornithology seminars and Canoeable Waterways of New York State and Vicinity written by Grinnell.
Creator:
Grinnell, Lawrence I. (Lawrence Irving), 1889-1985.
Grinnell, Lawrence I. (Lawrence Irving), 1889-1985. (Title of work: Canoeable waterways of New York State and vicinity..)
Quanitities:
7.3 cubic feet.
Language:
Collection material in English
BIOGRAPHICAL NOTE

Lawrence I. Grinnell was an ornithologist. He earned an M.S. in 1943 and a Ph.D. in 1947, both from Cornell University.
COLLECTION DESCRIPTION

Field journals; reports; lecture notes; data; films, photographs, an audiotape, and photograph albums; correspondence; financial records; maps; printed materials; and bird guides regarding ornithological trips taken by Lawrence Grinnell from 1938 to 1971: St. Lawrence Gulf, Churchill (Manitoba), Alaska, Southern and Western United States, Trinidad, Guatemala, Colombia, Mexico, West Indies, Tobago, British Guiana, Panama, Africa, New Zealand, Australia, the Amazon region, Brazil, Venezuela, Peru, the Antarctic, India, and Nepal; also, correspondence and a pamphlet, "The 1957-1958 New Zealand-Australia Expedition of the Laboratory of Ornithology"; materials pertaining to his dissertation A Study of the Common Redpoll a notebook of Cornell ornithology seminars and Canoeable Waterways of New York State and Vicinity.
RELATED MATERIAL

Lawrence I. Grinnell, "A Century of Unitarian History in Ithaca, 1865-1965." Pamphlet, #1525.
Correspondence and other material related to Lawrence Grinnell appears in the Arthur Augustus Allen papers, #21-18-1255 with whom he worked at the Laboratory of Ornithology.
