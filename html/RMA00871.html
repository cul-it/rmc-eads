DESCRIPTIVE SUMMARY

Title:
Seal and Serpent Society of Cornell University records, 1906-2009.
Repository:
Division of Rare and Manuscript Collections
Collection Number:
37-4-871
Abstract:
Materials relating to the Seal and Serpent, a local fraternity founded at Cornell in 1905.
Creator:
Seal and Serpent Society (Cornell University)
Quanitities:
12.5 cubic feet.
Language:
Collection material in English
ORGANIZATIONAL HISTORY

Local fraternity founded at Cornell in 1905, but the building is privately owned.
COLLECTION DESCRIPTION

Records include constitutions and bylaws, certificate of incorporation, 1919; correspondence, 1916-1965; Board of Directors', annual, and committee meeting minutes, 1906-1986; financial records, and construction plans and specifications; photographs, 1942-1991; many letters, accounts, plans, and other items relating to the presidency, beginning in 1923, of Creed Walsh Fulton ('09), during which a new house for the Society was planned, financed, and opened in 1927. Also, directories (incomplete run); directory questionnaires and personal data sheets; The Snake, the publication of the group; a printed petition, ca. 1907, to the executive board and chapters of Phi Kappa Sigma fraternity from the members of Seal and Serpent, asking for authority to establish a chapter of that fraternity at Cornell and including information concerning the fraternity system at Cornell at that time, a brief history of Seal and Serpent, a building fund prospectus, a group photograph, and a list of the petitioners with accompanying personal data; rush booklets; and miscellaneous items relating to the fraternity.
Also, group photograph including Alvin W. King (Class of 1907), one of the founding members of Seal and Serpent and bearer of pin number 1. CD with photographs of a ROTC event in September 2009.
Original bellows purchased as part of the original furnishings for the Lodge and selected by O. (Ornan) Hibbard Waltz, in 1927.
Access Restrictions:

Access restricted to written permission from the Society President, Vice President or Historian.
