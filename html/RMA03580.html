BIOGRAPHICAL NOTE

Professor Emeritus of Horticulture, Department of Horticultural Sciences at Geneva, NY. Areas of expertise include plant breeding, sweet cherry genetics, plum breeding, apricot breeding, variety testing, rootstock testing, and adult education relating to deciduous tree fruit production. From 1985 to 1990 he served as Professor and Chairman of the Department of Horticultural Sciences at NYS Agricultural Experiment Station, Geneva, New York. He retired July 14, 2004.

Scope and content

The collection contains correspondence, grant files, Horticultural Science Department planning documents (Geneva), McCarthy Farm Nursery documents, meetings and activities files, publications files, and New York plum pox virus files. Also correspondence, agreements and catalogs of international nurseries, stone fruit dispersals, "Ideas for Accessioning," correspondence for NY Growers, Cornell Research Foundation (CRF), USA Fruit Tree Nurseries, IPM, and Pear Cultivars and Culture.
