BIOGRAPHICAL NOTE

Barber B. Conable (b.1922) graduated from Cornell University in 1942, and was a Republican congressman representing the 37th Congressional District, New York in the U.S. House of Representatives until his retirement in 1985. He served as a New York senator, 1963-1964, before being elected to the 89th Congress.

Scope and content

Files of Barber Conable, generated during his tenure as New York State Senator and United States Congressman. Typescript journals, 1968-1984. Also, a narrative account of his experiences with the World Bank.
Scope and content

Also, a videotape of a speech given by Barber Conable at the University of Mississippi on March 30, 2000.
