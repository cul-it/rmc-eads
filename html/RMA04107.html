BIOGRAPHICAL NOTE

Professor of Music. David Borden was educated at the Eastman School of Music and Harvard University and was a Fulbright scholar in Berlin, studying at the Hochschule fur Musik. His first composition teachers were jazz musicians Jimmy Giuffre and Jaki Byard. He is the retired founder and Director of the Digital Music Program at Cornell. In 1969 he founded Mother Mallard's Portable Masterpiece Co., the world's first synthesizer ensemble, with the support of Robert Moog. His Continuing Story of Counterpoint, a twelve-part cycle of pieces for synthesizers, acoustic instruments, and voice has been called the "Goldberg Variations of minimalism." He also composed part of the soundtrack for The Exorcist (1973) and other films. His music is available on the Cuneiform, New World Records, Lameduck, and Arbiter labels.
COLLECTION DESCRIPTION

Original manuscript scores including "The Continuing Story of Counterpoint" (9 parts), "Dialogues", "Counterpoint for Flute, Cello, and Harp", 1978, "Enfield in Winter", "C-A-G-E", "I Trill Tunes", "Le Tombeau de Josquin", "CAIRN for JFK", "Trudymusic", "The Force (That Through the Green Fuse Drives the Flower)", "Heaven - Kept Soul", "Double Portrait", "Variations on a Theme of Philip Glass", "Quartet in Four Movements for Oboe and String Trio", "Twelve Preludes and Fugues".
CD's include "Earth Journeys", Vol. 1-12 and other works. Also, a poster depicting a performance of a composition written for a Moog synthesizer, and silk screens of 2 Mother Mallard Concerts and 1 self portrait.
Also, an unmixed master VTR cassette of David Borden and the Mother Mallard Band, September 16, 1988.
