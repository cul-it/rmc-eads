ORGANIZATIONAL HISTORY

The Bronfenbrenner Life Course Center is a university-wide umbrella organization that houses the Cornell Institute for Translational Research on Aging. Past institutes included the Cornell Employment and Family Careers Institute, the Cornell Gerontology Research Institute and the Pathways to Life Quality Study. The center is named in honor of Urie Bronfenbrenner, Professor Emeritus of Human Development and Psychology at Cornell.
COLLECTION DESCRIPTION

Includes historical information, committee meeting minutes, reports, publications and files concerning grants, symposiums and visiting scholars.

Series XII. Family Business Research Institute/ Cornell Employment and Family Careers Institute
Scope and Contents
During the mid to late 1990's, the Family Business Research Institute was a major institute within the Bronfenbrenner Life Course Center. FBRI was directed by Ramona Heck, J. Thomas Clark Professor of Entrepreneurship and Personal Enterprise. Ramona served on the Steering Committee for the Bronfenbrenner Life Course Center.
