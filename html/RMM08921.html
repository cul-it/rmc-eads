Content Description

The Mollie Gordon Camp Caravan Letters document the cross-country summer travel activities of camp organizer Mary "Mollie" Gertrude Gordon (Feb 2, 1914 – Sept 4, 2016), dating 1956–1972 (except 1962, when they didn't travel). It contains handwritten letters, travel itineraries, postcards, and a few photographs. These 16 journals, written in longhand by Mollie Gordon, describe daily events from Camp Caravan, a business venture she co-led with her husband, Bill Gordon. Each summer, they took approximately 25 boys aged 12–14 from New York City across the United States in two modified trucks named Hobo and Gypsy, camping nightly and visiting National Parks and other notable sites and locations, such as Old Forge and the Adirondacks (NY), Alaska, and more. The letters, written to Mollie's sisters, detail camping locations, meals, sightseeing, and the group's adventures and misadventures. A digital transcription of the 1956 letters and itinerary is included.
Biographical / Historical

Mary "Mollie" Gertrude Gordon was born on February 2, 1914, and passed away on September 4, 2016. A Cornell alumnus (class of 1936), Mollie co-founded Camp Caravan with her husband, Bill Gordon, a summer travel camp that operated from 1956 to 1972. The camp brought boys from New York City on cross-country journeys to experience camping and explore the natural beauty of the United States. Mollie documented these trips in detailed letters to her sisters, capturing the spirit and logistics of the camp's daily life. She traveled with her husband, their three children, and the campers in two converted cattle trucks, creating a unique and adventurous experience for all involved.
