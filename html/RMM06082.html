ORGANIZATIONAL HISTORY

The Methodist Episcopal Society organized in 1805 met in private homes until 1809 when a church building was erected. In 1834 a new building was constructed. Catharine was the center of Johnson's Settlement which the records indicate was a pivotal charge on the Catherine circuit. At vaious times the circuit also included: Newtown (Elmira), Hector, McIntyre's Settlement, North Settlement, Newtown Creek, Jefferson, Veteran, Horseheads, Dean's Corners and Newfield.
COLLECTION DESCRIPTION

This collection consists of one volume of Quarterly Conference meeting minutes from the Catharine circuit (1819-1847), a class book for the first class in Johnson's settlement (1833-39), and a record book from the Catharine Sunday School Society (1852-1882).

Item #2
Class book for the 1st Class in Johnson's Settlement for the year 1833",
 1833-1839
Scope and Contents
The volume contains a numbered list of members and their state in church and society; includes: their state in life, and their attendance for specific dates (1833-1839). At the end of the volume there follows a list of subscriptions (n.d.) and a list of members received on trial.
Item #3
Sabbath School Record Book,
 1852-1882
Scope and Contents
Includes constitution, by-laws and minutes of the Catharine Sunday School Association auxiliary to the Sunday School Union of the Methodist Episcopal Church. Annual minutes record the election of officers, the appointment of teachers, a financial report and the appointment and reports of committees.
