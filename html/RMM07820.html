BIOGRAPHICAL NOTE

American author and publisher, born in Muncie, Indiana in 1938. Mitchell was the founder of Calamus Books, an early small press devoted to gay male literature, and a cofounder of Gay Presses of New York in 1981. Mitchell also wrote fiction dealing with the gay male experience in New York City during the 1970s and 1980s, including the book of short stories My life as a mole (1989) and the novel The terminal bar (1982). The feature film Acid snow (1998) is based on Mitchell's novel of the same name. He died in Ithaca, New York in 2012.
COLLECTION DESCRIPTION

Mitchell's publications, drafts of his and other people's writings (including a draft of a Harvey Fierstein piece), video recording of Marsha P. Johnson, an African American drag queen who was a prominent participant in the 1969 Stonewall uprising in New York City and was an outspoken advocate for gay rights. Also includes interviews, reviews and awards for his writing (including the Lambda Literary Award) and the work of Calamus Books, as well as personal and family papers, including journals and diaries and a copy of Mitchell's baby book.
