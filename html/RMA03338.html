COLLECTION DESCRIPTION

Videocassettes, audio cassettes, press releases, tear sheets, publications, slides, photographs, and ephemera from the Communications Department of the Cornell University College of Veterinary Medicine, dating from ca. 1970-2002.
SERIES LIST

Series I. Audio and Video Cassettes

Boxes: 1, 7 -11
Several audio and video cassettes of varying formats. Most of the video cassettes are copies of stories run on television news or science programs featuring Cornell veterinarians. Some include local news coverage of college events. The audio tapes include recordings of college events as well as a number of American Association for the Advancement of Science program tapes. There are also 8 video tapes of interviews with several former deans of the college and then-president Hunter Rawlings which were recorded in 2000 for a promotional "Millennium" video that was never completed.
Series II. Publicity Subject Files

Boxes: 2 and 6
Press releases, tear sheets (news clippings), and miscellaneous publications containing references to college people, events and issues.
Series III. Photographs by Subject

Boxes: 2 - 4 and 9
Photographs, mostly from University Photography, arranged by subject; contains some slides.
Series IV. Photographs by Department

Boxes: 5 and 6
Negatives, contact sheets and some photos. Mostly portraits of faculty and staff arranged by department.
Series V. Ephemera

Boxes: 7
2 medallions created for the college centennial in 1994.
INFORMATION FOR USERS
Cite As:

Biographical / Historical

The Communications Department of the Cornell University College of Veterinary Medicine is responsible for publicity and external relations for the college. The college was known as the New York State College of Veterinary Medicine until the late 1980s.
Restrictions on Use:

Due to the fragility and potential degradation of moving image and sound recordings, viewing and listening is limited to items that have been digitized. If an item is in another media format, you may request to have the item digitized for access.
