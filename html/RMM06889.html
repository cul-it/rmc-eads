Scope and content

The Morse Chain Company records contains business records documenting the transactions of the Morse Chain Company and its subsidiary companies: the Thomas Morse Aircraft Corporation, the Barr-Morse Corporation, and the Peters-Morse Manufacturing Corporation, dating circa 1915-1975. The collection also contains bound volumes of the company newsletter the South Hill Echo and Re-Echo, and a poster: "The 'Rocker Joint,' Morse Genuine Silent Chains, from Automotive Industries, June 26, 1924.

Biographical / Historical

The Morse Manufacturing Company was established in 1892 and incorporated in 1898 in Trumansburg, NY as the Morse Chain Company. The company manufactured bicycle chains and went on to develop chain and power transmission equipment from its Ithaca, NY factory, built in 1906. Its subsidiaries, the Thomas Morse Aircraft Corporation (founded in 1917), the Peters-Morse Manufacturing Company (founded in 1919), and the Barr-Morse Corporation (founded in 1921, manufactured aircrafts, adding machines, and typewriters, respectively. The Morse Chain Company was taken over by the Borg-Warner Corporation in 1929.
