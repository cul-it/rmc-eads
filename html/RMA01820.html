DESCRIPTIVE SUMMARY

Title:
Cornell University Track and Field collection, ca. 1886-2000
Repository:
Division of Rare and Manuscript Collections
Collection Number:
40-9-1820
Abstract:
Materials documenting the history of the track teams at Cornell.
Creator:
Cornell University
Quanitities:
8 cubic feet.
Language:
Collection material in English
Materials documenting the history of the track teams at Cornell. Collection contains a collection of training logs and results books as well as various programs and ephemera relating to events and operations of the track team. Many photos and slides of individual team members and events, some photos include trips to England. A collection of medals won by Walker Smith is included as well as another set won by Daniel B. Strickler. Material from Los Angeles, Antwerp, Berlin and Oslo Summer Olympic Games material from Cornell Olympians including film footage and photos. Winning jersey bib number plates worn by winners in track and field at the 1920 Antwerp Belgium Olympics, this includes non-U.S. and non-Cornell winners as well, some are signed. Many films of various track events and training sessions. Films include, but are not limited to: IC4A and Heptagonal track and field events, 1932; Penn Relays, 1955; 1952 Olympics. Also a film primarily concerning Cornell athletics, including track and field and baseball that includes Spring Day games, a parade, male students clowning, and the Green Dragon. Also pictorial log of the trip to England of the track teams of Cornell University and Princeton University. The album has photographs, clippings, and memorabilia documenting the Anglo-American Athletic Match track and field events held in England between Cornell and Princeton and Oxford and Cambridge in July 1930. Also programs from the Niagara Track & Field Hall of Fame, featuring Cornellians.
NOTES

Walter Ashbaugh '51, Meredith Gourdine '52 and Charles H. Moore '51 competed at the 52 Olympics and may be featured on the film.

Scope and Contents
Several of the number plates are autographed by the athletes. The plates include Frank Foss, (Cornell '17) signed, Joseph Guillemot, Albert Hill signed (also appears signed by Horace Hallock Brown, Hannes Kolehmainen, Richmond Landon signed, Frank Loomis signed (also signed by Frank Sheehan Cornell trainer, Pat McDonald signed, Charley Paddock, Bevil Rudd, Patrick Ryan signed, and Earl Thomson. The one for Allen Woodring is missing. There are also two that have name labels that do not correspond with who won gold. They are shot put labeled "Finnthuma" actually won by Ville Pörhölä and broad jump labeled "Finna" actually won by William Petersson. Also includes a picture of Jack Moakley and Frank Sheehan and a U.S. Olympic track team patch. See photo on CD-1941 (located in Box 1, as well) for picture before it was disassembled.
