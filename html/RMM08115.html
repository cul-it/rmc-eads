Scope and content

Seven black books documenting different points in DOMS' long career, containing tags, fill-ins done in market, pen, paint pen, etc. with stickers, cut outs, and photographs glued in, and pages directly airbrushed. The books contain art by DOM and members of his Knock Out Crew, including associated writer DESA.
INFORMATION FOR USERS
Preferred Citation

Biographical / Historical

DOMS-KOC is a Harlem based graffiti artist who has been active since the 1980s. He is most well known for his airbrush work, especially on T-shirts.

Box 1
DOMS KOC Black Book #3
 1991
Scope and Contents
Contains writing from DOMS KOC, SHARIF, UPTOWN, THOR, and other artists, mostly associated with KOC. Most of the works are either tags or fill-ins. There's also an old check inside for Cleopatra Riley, and the sleeve of a DVD titled VideoGraf Volume One. Many pages are laminated with packing tape. Includes airbrushed foldout page with names of everyone in the Knock Out Crew.
Box 2
DOMS KOC Black Book #4
 2007
Scope and Contents
Contains writing and numerous stickers from NARC, BGA, SPLIT and many other writers. This black book is elaborate, some pieces take up multiple pages and utilize cut outs, name and postal stickers, etc. to create multi-media work. The pieces in this book also tend to be very vibrant. Contains airbrush, paint, marker, pen, etc. This black book is unusual because of the way the artists worked with form, it is very different from the other black books in DOMS' collection and shows the trajectory of his crew. An emphasis is on airbrushed works here. One page is dated 2007, and because of the style represented in this book compared to the 1990s "KOC style" it is fair to say most of it is from the mid-2000s.
