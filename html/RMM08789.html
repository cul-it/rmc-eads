DESCRIPTIVE SUMMARY

Title:
Kurt Vonnegut papers, 1950s-1970s
Repository:
Division of Rare and Manuscript Collections
Collection Number:
8789
Abstract:
Manuscripts and incoming correspondences relating to Kurt Vonnegut's work as a writer and materials relating to his family and personal life between the 1950s and 1970s.
Creator:
Vonnegut, Kurt.
Quanitities:
13.75 cubic feet.
The Kurt Vonnegut papers document Kurt Vonnegut's work as a writer and his personal life between the 1950s and 1970s. Items includes manuscript drafts, notebooks, letters and material from Vonneguts time with the General Electric Company.
Biographical / Historical

Kurt Vonnegut was born in Indianapolis on November 11, 1922. He was an American novelist best known for such works as Slaughterhouse-five, Cat's cradle, and God bless you, Mr. Rosewater. He died in Manhattan on April 11, 2007.

Biographical / Historical
Through the GI Bill, Vonnegut studied anthropology at the University of Chicago from 1945-1947. He received his graduate degree in anthropology 25 years after he left, when the University accepted his novel Cat's Cradle in lieu of his master's thesis in 1971.
