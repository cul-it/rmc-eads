Content Description

A teaching collection assembled by A.D. White about the medieval Church and the Lutheran Reformation. Contains 37 items, including four indulgences, the oldest being issued in 1259; a famous anti-Catholic print by the Lutheran artist Erhard Schön (1491-1542), titled "Klage Gottes über seinen Weinberg"; photographic image (printed on paper) titled, "Buddha as a Christian Saint at Palermo"; and the original porfolio case A.D. White used to carry materials to the classroom, which contains a list of items in his own handwriting. This material is a unique example of a small teaching collection with a selection of textual and visual materials ready for a class or a research seminar; it is an early example of how history was taught at Cornell in the 19th century. The archival documents themselves would be of interest to students and researchers in History, German studies, Religious Studies, as well as anyone interested in Cornell's early history.

Box 1 Folder 1
Letter ad executionem indulgentiarum
 1347-03-18
Original manuscript
Scope and Contents
From Heinrich Menger, expert in canon law (Kanoniker) from Zürich, commissioned by the Council of Basel (1346) to organize the dispensation of indulgences in the cities and dioceses of Constance, Bamberg, Würzburg, Augsburg, Eichstätt and Regensburg, in order to fund the council of reunification between the Western and Eastern Churches and to defray the costs of the delegation of the Greek Church and the Byzantine Emperor coming to Europe. Explains the procedure and appoints the Carthusian monk and abbot Johannes Rode to coordinate the project in, e.g., the vast diocese of Bamberg.

Scope and Contents
Printed in Augsburg in 1482 and issued by the Franciscan priest Petrus Cristannus, Subkommissar für den Türkenablass in Bavaria. Facsimile. The (printed) original document is kept Rare Books BX2282 A2 1482 Broadside box + The letter of indulgence was printed many times, with almost identical wording, in the years 1482 and 1483, mostly on behalf of these individual commissaries. Outside the Vatican, only seven copies of 1482/3 indulgences are preserved today: one is kept in Freiburg; one in the Bayerische Staatsbibliothek; one at the Bodleian (Oxford); one at the University of Pennsylvania; one in the Huntington Library; one in private hands; and one at Cornell.

Box 1 Folder 20
Erhard Schön (1491-1542), "Klage Gottes über seinen Weinberg"
 1532
Original woodcut, 262 X 465 mm.
Scope and Contents
Similar engraving kept at the British Library, but with 140 lines of verse by Hans Sachs. This is satire of the decadent and corrupt Church of Rome, by a Lutheran artist. Three clerics are trying to escape a fenced-in vineyard guarded by a huge dog. Behind them, the Pope as well as monks are uprooting and burning stems and use the blood of Christ (who appears crucified on a vine) to grow a dead tree. Papal indulgences and other symbols of the alleged corruption of the Roman Catholic Church hand from the branches of these trees. By contrast, on the left, a devout congregation is listening to a Protestant preacher who celebrates a culture of life.
