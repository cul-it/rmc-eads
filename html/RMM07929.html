Manuscript Box 86
"Respect à l'enfance! Guerre à l'immoralité!"
 1914
Postcard
Scope and Contents
Promotional postcard for the Ligue Nationale contre la Licence des Etalages et l'Immoralitié, a Belgian national organization that lobbied to protest the display and sale of what it characterized as "des images, des livres, des objets contraires aux bonnes moeurs."
Manuscript Box 86
Mademoiselle A. Castens promotional card
 1910
Ephemera
Scope and Contents
Promotional card for the fitness practice of Mademoiselle A. Castens, a pioneer in offering physical training for women and children whose salon was located on the swanky boulevard Haussmann. The reference to "réspiration normale" no doubt represents pushback against the fashion of tight corsets for women, which were increasingly seen as a threat to health. The card features high-style fine-press art nouveau typography and decorative graphics by Graveur Stern, a leading society engraver and printer in business in the Passage des Panoramas in Paris from 1834 to 2015.
