BIOGRAPHICAL NOTE

David Lyons is an authority an legal philosophy and has written extensively on utilitarianism and the moral aspects of legal theory. He received a B.A. from Brooklyn College, and an M.A. and Ph.D. from Harvard University. He also completed a Knox Fellowship at Oxford University. He came to Cornell in 1964, received a Guggenheim Fellowship in 1970, became a full professor in 1971, and served as Chairman of the Philosophy Department (Sage School of Philosophy) from 1978 to 1984. He also began teaching in the Law School in 1979. He retired from Cornell in 1995, and continues to teach legal philosophy at Boston University.
