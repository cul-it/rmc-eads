BIOGRAPHICAL NOTE

In 1983, Richard A. Heyman (1935-1994) was elected mayor of Key West, Florida, and became the first openly gay mayor in the United States. He moved to Key West in 1973 and opened Gingerbread Square Art Gallery. Heyman is best remembered for his work winning a second city-wide referendum that lead to Key West's first wastewater treatment facility.

INFORMATION FOR USERS
Cite As:

Richard Heyman Papers, #7770. Division of Rare and Manuscript Collections, Cornell University Library.
Scope and content

Scrapbooks, campaign material, speeches, photographs, political buttons, and other material chronicling Heyman's political career, collected by his staff assistant June Keith and campaign manager Peter Ilchuk. Also a copy of John Mikytuck's documentary about Heyman and the early AIDS crisis in Key West, "The Newcomer."
