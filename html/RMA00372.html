Personal papers of de Kiewiet consisting of correspondence to and from de Kiewiet in South Africa, Rhodesia, London, Paris, Berlin, Iowa City, and Ithaca; also, lecture notes, records of Cornell University's Army Intensive Language Program, a diploma, photographs, and miscellaneous reflective and creative writings.

BIOGRAPHICAL NOTE

Professor, dean, provost, and acting president of Cornell University, 1941-51; subsequently president of the University of Rochester.
COLLECTION DESCRIPTION

Personal papers of de Kiewiet consisting of correspondence to and from de Kiewiet in South Africa, Rhodesia, London, Paris, Berlin, Iowa City, and Ithaca; also, lecture notes, records of Cornell University's Army Intensive Language Program, a diploma, photographs, and miscellaneous reflective and creative writings.
Letter from C. W. de Kiewiet to Deane W. Malott, December 30, 1960, replying to a letter of sympathy on the death of de Kiewiet's daughter. de Kiewiet recalls Paul Tillich, while talking about the international conflict, saying that the great efforts that we are making to maintain ourselves are also the great efforts which may destroy us.
