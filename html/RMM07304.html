DESCRIPTIVE SUMMARY

Title:
Harry Langhorne papers, 1972-1977.
Repository:
Division of Rare and Manuscript Collections
Collection Number:
7304
Abstract:
The collection consists of the papers of Harry Langhorne, a Philadelphia area gay activist and executive officer of the Gay Activists Alliance of Philadelphia. Includes subject files relating to all aspects of the gay movement and of other liberal and radical social causes.
Creator:
Langhorne, Harry.
Quanitities:
5 cubic feet.
Language:
Collection material in English
BIOGRAPHICAL NOTE

Harry Langhorne was an executive administrator of the Gay Activists Alliance of Philadelphia (GAA) from 1972 to 1977.
COLLECTION DESCRIPTION

The collection consists of the subject files kept by Langhorne while at the Gay Activists Alliance (GAA), as well as his correspondence, notes, drafts of essays, and other accumulated printed material. The committees referred to in the files are those of GAA. Topics include gay legal rights and legislation, sodomy laws, gay politics including especially gay Marxism, bi- and transsexuality, gay media concerns, sex education, homophobia, special gay interest groups such as handicapped gays, and gay-church issues. The collection documents relations between CAA and the gay liberation movement and Philadelphia, the State of Pennsylvania, and national political governments, especially pertaining to their statues and practices relating to the gay movement. GAA communications with other regional and national gay organizations and many of their publications are included.
