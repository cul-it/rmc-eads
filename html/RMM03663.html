BIOGRAPHICAL NOTE

Artist.
Alison Mason Kingsbury was an artist in Ithaca, N.Y. She came to Ithaca in 1926 as a student assistant to Ezra Winter, who designed the mural in the lobby of Willard Straight Hall, Cornell University. She married Morris Bishop, a noted Cornell writer and historian.
COLLECTION DESCRIPTION

Drawings, illustrations, and paintings; charcoal landscape drawings; exhibition catalogs and reviews; bound volumes of sketches, notebooks and pamphlets. Also, "Unheralded Artist: Alison Kingsbury," Princeton University undergraduate thesis by Holly Markovitz, 2000.
RELATED MATERIALS

See also Alison Mason Kingsbury Gannett Clinic mural materials, #43-32-1193, Alison Jolly Papers, #8517, and Morris Bishop Papers, #14-18-641. A video of Kingsbury is availible in the Cornell Artists and Local Video Programs collection, #3867.
