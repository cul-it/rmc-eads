Biographical / Historical

Jorge "PopMaster Fabel" Pabon is a celebrated dancer and artist from Spanish Harlem, NY, noted for his contributions to Hip Hop dance styles, particularly popping and locking. He appeared as a dancer in the 1984 feature film Beat Street and is a former Senior Vice President of the b-boying crew and hip hop group Rock Steady Crew. He is a co-founder of GhettOriginal Productions, Inc. with which he co-authored, co-directed, and co-choreographed the first two Hip Hop musicals ever produced, "So! What Happens Now?" and "Jam on the Groove" (first official Off-Broadway Hip Hop musical). He is a Hip Hop dance instructor at New York University.
Scope and content

Posters, flyers, news clippings, programs and other ephemera documenting the life and work of Jorge "Popmaster Fabel" Pabon. Materials document his life as a b-boy and active participant in hip hop culture from the early 1980s onward. Included in the collection are video tapes of rehearsals and performances, along with a black book of art work begun in 1981 while Fabel was in High School.
