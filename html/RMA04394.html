Content Description

Collection documents aspects of Greek arts, music, rebetika, literature, and politics. Publications, photographs, interviews, and correspondence relating to Mikis Theodorakis, Nikos Kavadias, and Iakōvos Kampanellēs, amongst others. Also recordings of Greek music. Also poetry related items including correspondence with Jon Stallworthy. Angelakē-Rouk, Katerina. Correspondence between Miss Ella Dunkel and Netty Palmer. Articles and other materials related to the Greek Junta and Holst-Warhaft's work with the anti-junta organizations in Australia and elsewhere.
Biographical / Historical

Gail Holst-Warhaft served as an adjunct Professor in Classics, Comparative Literature and Near Eastern Studies at Cornell University. She also served as the Director of the Mediterranean Studies Initiative and Faculty Associate of the Cornell Center for a Sustainable Future. Areas of focus include Modern Greek Literature and Music and Greek Literature from Antiquity to the Present. She is also a poet, translator, and musician.
