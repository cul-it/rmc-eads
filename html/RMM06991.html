DESCRIPTIVE SUMMARY

Title:
Gail and Stephen Rudin New York landmarks collection, 1835-1944.
Repository:
Division of Rare and Manuscript Collections
Collection Number:
6991
Abstract:
Paper ephemera, postcards, and photographs related to the Brooklyn Bridge and other New York landmarks. Includes booklet, program, invitation, and ticket to opening ceremony for bridge on May 24, 1883. Also includes receipt for the purchase of Randall's Island, as well as miscellany related to the Holland Tunnel and the Statue of Liberty.
Creator:
Rudin, Stephen
Quanitities:
.3 cubic feet.
Language:
Collection material in English
COLLECTION DESCRIPTION

Paper ephemera, postcards, and photographs related to the Brooklyn Bridge and other New York landmarks. Includes booklet, program, invitation, and ticket to opening ceremony for bridge on May 24, 1883. Also includes receipt for the purchase of Randall's Island, as well as miscellany related to the Holland Tunnel and the Statue of Liberty.

SEPARATED MATERIAL

Books
  1. Brooklyn Bridge 1883-1933. A valuable history published to commemorate the 50th anniversary of the Brooklyn Bridge. First Edition.
  2. Opening Ceremonies of the New York and Brooklyn Bridge. 1883. Inscribed on flyleaf by Emily Warren Roebling, wife of Chief Engineer Washington Roebling. Rudin # M-48A
  3. The New York City Subway: Its Construction and Equipment. Inscription reads "Presented to Alderman John H. Dougherty, by August Belmont, Esq. President, Interborough Rapid Transit Company, November 21st, 1904". Rudin # M-94
