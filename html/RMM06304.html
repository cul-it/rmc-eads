DESCRIPTIVE SUMMARY

Title:
James Reeves-Laura (Riding) Jackson correspondence, 1933-1940.
Repository:
Division of Rare and Manuscript Collections
Collection Number:
6304
Abstract:
Letters, mostly written by Laura (Riding) Jackson to James Reeves, concerning collaborative literary projects, the magazine The Vulgate (Epilogue), and a literary group formed in Majorca (Spain) in the 1930s. In her letters, Laura (Riding) Jackson provides lengthy and detailed criticism of James Reeves' poetry.
Creator:
Jackson, Laura (Riding), 1901-1991.
Reeves, James.
Quanitities:
.5 cubic feet.

COLLECTION DESCRIPTION

Letters, mostly written by Laura (Riding) Jackson to James Reeves, concerning collaborative literary projects, the magazine The Vulgate (Epilogue), and a literary group formed in Majorca (Spain) in the 1930s. In her letters, Laura (Riding) Jackson provides lengthy and detailed criticism of James Reeves' poetry.
INFORMATION FOR USERS
Cite As:

James Reeves-Laura (Riding) Jackson correspondence, #6304. Division of Rare and Manuscript Collections, Cornell University Library.
Biographical / Historical

James Reeves (pseudonym of John Morris, 1909-1978), poet, critic, and author of children.s stories. Laura (Riding) Jackson (1901-1991), poet and critic. The collection was purchased by Cornell University in 1989 and was originally part of Laura (Riding) Jackson and Schuyler B. Jackson collection#4608.
